How Much Does a Worcester Boiler Service Cost?

Assuming your Worcester or Worcester Bosch boiler is functioning normally without any known faults, a standard service typically costs around £120, including VAT. Insights from an Engineer’s Perspective

As engineers, we cannot emphasize enough the importance of having all your gas appliances, particularly older ones like boilers, checked annually. If you’re unsure about the age of your boiler, you can search for the make and model on the internet or refer to any available paperwork. While servicing is generally feasible, even reputable brands like Worcester have a lifespan of 16 to 20 years. Regarding repairs, we recommend that customers spend a maximum of £400 on repairing a 10-year old wall-hung combi boiler, or £650 on repairing a 10-year-old Worcester Highflow model. Both of these boiler types typically have plenty of life left in them at that age. However, if your boiler is nearing 16 years old and the required repairs are projected to cost significantly more, it might be worth considering replacing your old Worcester boiler with a newer, A-rated model. Not only will this potentially save you money on energy bills, but it will also ensure reliable and efficient performance.

It’s important to remember that engineers prioritize safety above all other considerations, including the customer’s immediate heating and hot water needs. They adhere to the regulations set by the Gas Safe Register. Therefore, if an engineer identifies defects or rectification work that needs to be carried out, or if changes are required to comply with newer regulations, these recommendations are made for valid reasons.
